# Author - Georg Simmel

## Bio
Georg Simmel (1858–1918) was a German sociologist and philosopher who wrote on urban life, the [[Concept - Money Economy]], and modernity. A founding figure of sociology, his neo-Kantian approach helped establish sociological antipositivism; he pioneered analyses of individuality and social fragmentation. Through "The Metropolis and Mental Life" he was a precursor of urban sociology, symbolic interactionism, and social network analysis, and an acquaintance of Max Weber.

## Key Ideas
- The metropolis and mental life; [[Concept - Blasé]] attitude.
- [[Concept - Calculative Rationality]] and objective vs. subjective spirit in modern culture.
